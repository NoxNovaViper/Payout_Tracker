About this ledger

This page tracks publicly confirmed settlements and court judgments paid to resolve U.S. police misconduct claims — wrongful deaths, wrongful shootings, wrongful convictions, and failures to provide care in custody. Every figure links to its source.

What the totals mean

  • "This year" sums confirmed cases dated in the current calendar year.
  • "All‑time (tracked)" sums every confirmed case in this ledger — it is not a claim about every settlement ever paid in the U.S. Most payouts are small, undisclosed, or simply never covered by press; independent analyses have put the true national total in the billions over the past decade alone.

How it stays current

A scraper bundled with this site's source code scans public news coverage for new settlement reports roughly every 12 hours and lists candidates under "Newly spotted." Nothing moves into the confirmed ledger — or into the totals above — without a human checking the figure against a primary source first. That's a deliberate choice: a wrong dollar amount next to a real person's name is worse than a slower update.

Independence

This is a self-hosted, open project. It is not produced or operated by any AI company, law firm, or government body, and it does not speak for any of the people or families named on it.
